mapo toufu

Description

Mapo Tofu is a classic Sichuan dish featuring soft tofu cooked in a spicy, numbing sauce made with minced pork or beef, fermented black beans, doubanjiang (spicy bean paste), and Sichuan peppercorns. It has a rich, savoury, and fiery flavour with a signature tingling sensation from the peppercorns. Traditionally served over steamed rice, it’s a staple of Sichuan cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 300g soft or silken tofu, cut into cubes
  • 150g minced pork (or beef)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 2 spring onions, chopped(for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare ingredients – Cut tofu into cubes and mix cornstarch with water.
  2. Blanch tofu (optional but helps texture) – Gently simmer tofu in hot salted water for 2 minutes, then drain.
  3. Sauté aromatics – Heat oil in a pan, fry garlic, ginger, and fermented black beans.
  4. Cook the meat – Add minced pork/beef and stir-fry until browned.
  5. Add seasonings – Stir in doubanjiang, soy sauce, sugar, and Sichuan peppercorns.
  6. Simmer – Pour in stock, then add tofu and gently stir. Let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Thicken the sauce – Add cornstarch slurry and mix until slightly thickened.
  8. Garnish & serve – Sprinkle with chopped spring onions and extra Sichuan pepper. Serve hot with rice!
